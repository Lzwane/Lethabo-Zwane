SMU celebrates 10 years of Academic Excellence

May 22, 2024

Sefako Makgatho Health Sciences University (SMU) celebrated its 10th anniversary on May 17, 2024, at the Sports Complex. The event marked a decade of academic excellence and highlighted the university's impact on society. The institution's commitment to transforming health services through excellence and innovation was praised. Congratulatory messages from dignitaries, including the Director General of the Department of Higher Education, Dr Nkosinathi Sishi, acknowledged SMU's contributions to higher education, health, science, and technology. SMU Council Chairperson, Ms Maria Rambauli, expressed hope for a bright future, citing stakeholders' continued support. Established in 2014, SMU is a specialized health sciences university with approximately 7,000 students enrolled in its five schools.

Appointment of Tito Titus Mboweni as the chancellor

May 10, 2024

The Council of Sefako Makgatho Health Sciences University (SMU) has elected and appointed Mr Tito Titus Mboweni as Chancellor for a five-year term. As Chancellor, Mr Mboweni will confer degrees, awards, and other distinctions on behalf of the University. He will also perform other functions assigned by the Council. Mr Mboweni has an impressive profile, including:

- Former Minister of Finance and Labour
- Former Governor of the South African Reserve Bank
- Chairperson of various companies and organizations
- Honorary Professor at several universities
- Recipient of multiple honorary doctorates

The Council and Chairperson, MS MM Rambauli, extend their gratitude to Mr Mboweni for accepting this role and wish him success.

Instruction When Operating The Lifts

May 8, 2024

1.Please do not jump up and down in the lift.
2.Do not stand in the doorway for too long.
3.If the door is closing, let it close, do not put any resistance against it.
4.Be careful when loading, do not bump the lift.
5.Do not press the buttons repeatedly.
6.Wait for people to exit before boarding.
7.Do not use the lift in circumstances of fire.
8.When stuck in the lift, please press the alarm button, and wait for assistance or call the emergency number given.
9.Do not attempt to break the doors.
10. Do not throw objects into the lift shaft.

Incidents of Alleged Fraudulent Medical Certificates Submitted By Certain Students

April 29, 2024

The Office of the Registrar has received reports of students submitting fraudulent medical certificates to defer assessments or gain concessions. These incidents include:
- Students impersonating medical practitioners or submitting certificates from non-existent practitioners
- Submitting certificates from unregistered practitioners or those not qualified to issue medical certificates
- Submitting certificates when the student was fit to take the assessment
Submitting fraudulent medical certificates is academic misconduct, a crime under the Health Professions Act, and can lead to:
- Disciplinary action under the Student Code
- Criminal charges and imprisonment or fines
- Damage to career prospects
The Registrar has initiated an investigation and will work with Deans to curb this practice.
Students are urged to seek support from university structures instead of resorting to fraudulent behavior.
